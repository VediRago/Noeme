# Scene — Wedding Collapse

Status:
- Scene blueprint
- Prose exists
- Adaptation/script version not written

- Act 01 — Lucreta

Part:
- Part 01 — Lucreta

Source:
- Lucreta prose

Characters:
- Lucreta
- Vera

Location:
- Victorian-inspired aristocratic ceremonial bedroom
- Mirror area leading toward the door

Purpose:
- Complete the progression from controlled wedding performance into undeniable body failure.
- Let Lucreta almost reach the ceremony before the condition takes the moment from her.
- Preserve the mirror/reflection language as her control begins to fail.
- Show that Vera notices too late to prevent the collapse.

Scene Summary:
Lucreta recovers only enough from the blood and cough to continue. She straightens, gathers her gown with practiced grace, and turns away from the mirror toward the door. The camera does not immediately follow her; it stays with the mirror as her reflection leaves with her.

As she moves, her body gives a small warning. She gently stumbles on her own foot, almost like a drunken imbalance. The missed step is caught mostly in the reflection rather than directly on her body, making it feel as though the reflection itself falters. Lucreta does not register the stumble, or refuses to register it. She keeps moving forward, breathing slowly and trying to survive the moment without breaking the performance.

Vera notices the imbalance and calls her name as a check, not panic. Lucreta reaches the polished brass handle. Her fingers begin to slip. Vera realizes Lucreta is not okay and calls again, more desperate. Lucreta’s knee connects with the floor. There is no meaningful pause between the fingers slipping, the knee hitting, and the rest of the body falling. Vera’s final call is almost shouted and heavily muffled from Lucreta’s perspective.

Lucreta falls from the knee position. The camera follows the fall from knee level. Her head/body strikes the floor, and the collapse finally becomes undeniable. Her pale-mint eyes remain fixed and hollow. The camera holds on her eyes, then pulls back as Vera rushes into frame and comes to her aid.

World / Design Language:
- The room remains Victorian-inspired, aristocratic, ceremonial, dim, and controlled.
- Beauty and ritual should still be present, but the body failure breaks through them.
- The polished brass handle matters because Lucreta nearly reaches the ceremony before losing control.
- The mirror remains important even as she leaves it; reflection language carries the first visible falter.

Emotional Function:
- Start emotion: controlled continuation after the blood/cough moment.
- Shift: subtle physical failure becomes impossible to hide.
- End emotion: collapse, helplessness, and Vera’s delayed realization.
- Reader/viewer should feel: the moment is taken from Lucreta just before she reaches it.

Character Function:
- Lucreta:
  - Still tries to preserve the ceremony and her composure.
  - Does not openly surrender to weakness.
  - Her body fails faster than her performance can adapt.
- Vera:
  - Notices the imbalance too late.
  - Her concern escalates from check to desperation.
  - She has no clean rescue window once Lucreta’s fingers slip.

Structural Function:
- Completes the Lucreta wedding sequence.
- Turns the mirror/condition imagery into physical collapse.
- Establishes that small symptoms can become catastrophic quickly.
- Makes the wedding doorway a failed threshold, later inverted by Parents leaving the church.

Visual / Camera Function:
- The camera should not immediately follow Lucreta when she turns from the mirror.
- The shot stays with the mirror long enough for her reflection to leave with her.
- The stumble should be caught mostly in reflection, not directly on Lucreta.
- The fall should feel fast once the fingers slip, even though the blueprint separates the motion into readable anchors.
- The camera follows the fall from knee level.
- Hold on Lucreta’s fixed pale-mint eyes before pulling back to Vera rushing in.

- Lucreta hides or denies weakness until her body fails too fast for Vera to stop it.

- The collapse should feel like control vanishing all at once after several small warnings.

Important Notes:
- Do not make Vera seem slow or negligent.

- The important idea is that the mirror/reflection language breaks with her body.
- The collapse should feel sudden, not melodramatically extended.
