# 03_Parents — Part 1

## Purpose

This part establishes Philip and Abigail's relationship.

It should feel warm, playful, and alive before the later parts place the family under pressure.

The reader should understand their love through how they speak to each other, tease each other, and keep the conversation moving.

## Characters

- Philip / Phil
- Abigail / Abby
- Sera

## Structure

Leaving Church → Walk to Car → Ride to Lake → Lake

The movement should feel simple and ordinary. The point is not a large plot event. The point is letting the reader spend time with the family rhythm.

## Important Details

### Leaving Church

This moment inverts Lucreta's ending.

Lucreta's life ends at a doorway before the life she hoped for can begin.

Philip and Abigail's life together begins by opening a door from the church into the city.

The echo should not be explained directly. It is enough that the image quietly mirrors Lucreta in the opposite direction.

### Lake

The lake is the first major relationship scene.

It should show Philip and Abigail as people who already know how to play with each other emotionally.

This is where their affection should feel natural, not announced.

## Prose Notes

Dialogue and interaction should carry the scene.

The tone should be lighter than Lucreta, but not empty. The warmth here matters because it gives later loss something real to take away.
