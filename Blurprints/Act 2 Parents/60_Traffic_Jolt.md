Purpose:
- Break the reflective silence established in 50_Ride_Mood_Shift.
- Reassert the city's constant presence without needing a special dramatic incident.
- Remind the audience that personal moments must compete with public pressure.
- Pull Phil and Abby back into the physical world of the city.

Emotional Function:
- Start emotion: reflective silence after Abby's sky/fog line.
- Shift: abrupt physical interruption from traffic.
- End emotion: grounded reality and resumed movement.
- Reader/viewer should feel: the city never truly lets go of them.

Character Function:
- Phil:
  - Reacts instinctively to traffic.
  - Returns to practical driving concerns.
  - Keeps control without turning the moment into a crisis.
- Abby:
  - Is pulled out of her reflection by the traffic jolt.
  - Returns attention to the immediate world around them.
  - Does not need to explain the previous silence.

Structural Function:
- Follows directly after 50_Ride_Mood_Shift, where silence takes control of the car.
- Interrupts the private reflective pocket with ordinary city pressure.
- Keeps the Parents act grounded in the city as an ongoing force rather than isolated events.
- Bridges the quiet car moment into the next movement of the trip.

World / Scene Texture:
- The interruption should come from normal city density: too many people, too many vehicles, too much noise.
- No special accident, protest, attack, or plot event is needed.
- The city itself is the pressure.
- Brake lights, packed lanes, crowded sidewalks, horns, fogged glass, and bodies near the road should carry the scene.

Visual / Camera Function:
- Let silence hold briefly before the jolt.
- The brake should feel sudden but ordinary.
- Use the line of brake lights and stopped vehicles to show congestion stretching ahead.
- Keep the outside world close to the car windows.

Scene Summary:
Silence fills the car after Abby's line about the sky and the fog. For a brief moment, the private quiet holds. Then traffic thickens ahead. Vehicles slow in uneven waves, brake lights multiplying through the fog. Phil notices the road stopping and brakes sharply.

The jolt pulls Abby out of her thoughts. Both of them look forward. There is no special accident or dramatic event ahead, only the city doing what the city always does: too many vehicles, too many people, too much noise, and too little space. The road is locked with congestion, pedestrians, fog, lights, and movement.

Phil steadies the car and the moment. The private pocket created in the previous scene is broken, and the city noise pushes back in. The ride continues through congestion, carrying them toward the next travel beat without resolving Abby's uncertainty.
