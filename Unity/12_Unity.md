  written by
Diogo Oliveira


INT. DEVAAR FACILITY — LOWER LEVEL — CONTINUOUS

Waiting for the elevator to rise from the basement, KAEL
stands with her arms crossed while SERA paces past her.

                         SERA
               I told you.

Sera stops long enough to knock her fist against the wall.

                            SERA (CONT'D)
               I knew it.

She turns back to Kael.

                         SERA (CONT'D)
               Didn't I tell you these people
               were no good?

She starts pacing again.

                         SERA (CONT'D)
               Don't. Keep it to yourself.

Kael drops her arms and lowers her head.

                            SERA
               If they...

Sera stops and kicks the wall.

                         SERA (CONT'D)
               If they did anything to her...

The elevator arrives.

                         SERA (CONT'D)
               Come on. Let's go.

They step inside.

The descent is quick.

Too quick.

At the bottom, Sera turns to Kael.

                         SERA
               Don't worry, okay?


She places a hand against Kael's face and looks straight
into her eyes.

                          SERA (CONT'D)
                Whatever happens, it's us
                against them.

Then, as she turns back, she glances over her shoulder with
a smile and lifts her thumb.

                          SERA (CONT'D)
                We got this.

The doors open before they reach them.

Inside, a few scientists stop what they are doing.

FEDRA slowly lifts her head from VALKARE's chest.

For a few seconds, no one moves.

Sera sees Fedra first.

Then Valkare.

Then the room around them, full of machines and people who
suddenly look guilty.

                          SERA
                WHAT HAVE YOU DONE TO HER?

She walks straight toward them.

                          SERA (CONT'D)
                What did you do?

Kael enters behind her.

Slower.

Her eyes drift to the side.

ERIT's body lies connected by the same cable she once had.

A small shift of color moves through the room.

Then everything pauses.

The sound goes first.


Motion remains, but only barely.

Fedra's mouth opens without a voice.

Sera's arm lifts through water.

Valkare turns too slowly.

Kael sees the cable.

The body.

The face.

Then motion snaps back.

Kael is screaming.

Everyone falls.

Bodies hit the floor as sound returns all at once.

Then nothing moves.

Kael drops to her knees.

Sera is on the floor.

She crawls toward her.

Her hands slip once, then catch against Sera's clothes.

She pulls herself closer and holds her.

No answer.

No movement.

Kael looks at Sera's face.

Her body recoils backward until her spine strikes the
machine.

                                          CUT TO:

INT. DEVAAR FACILITY — MAINFRAME — CONTINUOUS

It's the seams on the floor that guide her sight toward
SERA’S body just ahead.


Her back against the mainframe. Arms braced around her
knees.

Fingers slowly curl into a fist.

Her arm rises.

Her lips form the shape of a word Sera taught her.

I—

She slams it.

The impact launches her body forward. Tears scatter with
it.

I—

She lifts her head. A tear slides across her cheek as she
opposes it.

Her eyes open.

U—

She slams it again. Her fist detaches it from her face. The
back of her head hits the machine.

rolling until it stops at the connection port on her arm.

Her eyes gather the distance.

She is already lifting herself toward the table.

Her fingers slide gently over Erit's arm until they reach
the cable.

In one motion — turning hers while disconnecting Erit's.

She connects it to herself.

                                          — UNITY —

INT. DEVAAR HOUSE — ERIT'S ROOM — [FRAGMENT]

Fingers adjusting fabric slipping from a shoulder.

                           ERIT'S MOTHER
                 Erit, stop shifting. You know


               this is important for your
               father.

A slight tap against her head.

                         ERIT'S MOTHER (CONT'D)
               Your family.

                         ERIT
               I just don't like these places.
               People are—

Weird.

                            KAEL
               ...weird.

Her mother gently pushes the hair behind her ear.

                         ERIT'S MOTHER
               You're spoiled, you know that?

Pulling her head back—

                            ERIT (CONT'D)
               I'll stay.

                            KAEL
               Stay...

Erit pushes her mother away as she walks off.

Peeking behind a wall.

                            ABBY
               Sera!

A sudden shift. Now the ceiling.

                         ABBY (CONT'D)
               Come down right this moment,
               young girl. Don't make me go
               there.

Kael tries extending her arm—

There is no movement.

A gasp.


Her hands fail to reach her throat.

There is no throat.

No breath.

Nothing.

She surrenders. A familiar woman towers closer with each
step.

                         ABBY (CONT'D)
               Where were you?

Abby's eyes lock into Sera's before moving away.

                            SERA
               Dumdum!

                            KAEL
               Dumdum...

Abby glances back—

                         ABBY
               School day, you know that!

                            KAEL
               I don't...

                         SERA
               I don't wanna!

Kael starts to fall but there is nothing to fall to—

                         VERA
               ...fearful of womanhood at the
               final hour, dear. You linger so
               long one might think this
               marriage burdens you terribly.

A reflection she has seen countless times.

                         LUCRETA
               No, Vera. Quite the contrary.

                         KAEL
               Quite the contrary.


With a smile that simply persists.

                         LUCRETA
               Nothing could mar this day.
               There is nothing—

                         KAEL
               ...There is nothing...

A cut. Hard. Someone else. Somewhere else entirely.

[FRAGMENT — DISTANT]

Shoving a man against a wall. The tip of a gun presses
against his chest.

                         UNKNOWN VOICE
               Hey pretty guy, those pockets
               full?

                         KAEL
               Hey pretty guy those pockets...

The barrel slides across his face. Stops between the eyes.

                         KAEL (CONT'D)
               The wall needs painting. I
               wouldn't waste...

                         UNKNOWN VOICE
               The wall needs painting. I
               don't want to ruin it with a
               shit brain.

A blow. Falling to her knees.

                           UNKNOWN VOICE (CONT'D)
               Julia lo—

Gone.

[FRAGMENT — FURTHER]

A sword thrusts out of a body. She reels in.

                         UNKNOWN VOICE
               Does this mean it's a big one,
               dad?


                           KAEL
                 Does this mean...

Gone.

[FRAGMENT — NOISE]

Staring at a bright sky.

A reflection in a window.

A crown of flowers.

A bloody hand.

A ripple in—

A light.

Hands.

Cars.

People.

Light.

Black.

White.

                            DOCTOR (V.O.)
                 Grey...

INT. HOSPITAL — EXAMINATION ROOM —

The ophthalmoscope lowers from her eye.

                           DOCTOR
                 Never seen those before.

He slides his chair back to stand.

                           DOCTOR (CONT'D)
                 And it's not even grey. It's
                 pale.

Shifting his shoulder while turning away.

                            DOCTOR (CONT'D)


                  I gotta say, you are quite the
                  miracle, young lady.

THE GIRL looks at her feet. Noise of life calling her
attention.

                            THE GIRL
                  So I'm okay?

She moves her sight to the window.

                            THE GIRL (CONT'D)
                  How long have I been here
                  anyway?

His eyes lift from the folder toward her. He slides a pen
into his pocket.

                              DOCTOR
                  Two days.

He walks toward the door.

                            DOCTOR (CONT'D)
                  You can go home now.

A hop. A few steps. She is at the window.

The people.

The traffic.

The stoplights.

Cellphones.

Cars.

Light.

                            THE GIRL
                  I get it now.

She lifts her head to see the bright sky.

The doctor glances over his shoulder, stopping as he is
about to leave.

                              THE GIRL (CONT'D)


It never ends.

                 — RETURN —
