# **TEASER — "NOEME"**

*Duration: ~20 seconds. No dialogue. No narration.*

*The teaser is the show's premise disguised as a title card. It teaches the audience what FRAGMENTS is about before they know they are being taught.


**TEASER — COLD OPEN**

BLACK.

Complete void. No sound. No light.

Then — very slowly — letters begin to appear. Blurred. Almost unreadable. They shimmer at the edge of visibility.

*\[ The letters spell: NOEME. They are distorted, as though seen through water or fog. The audience cannot fully read them yet. \]*

Beneath the letters, barely visible: the word PROTOTYPE. Also blurred. Also incomplete.

Then the music hits.

*\[ The music arrives at the same instant the image does — a face, or two faces, layered. Dark hair, red hair. Eyes that catch light. The image fades in as the letters sharpen. Both things happen at once. \]*

As the image resolves, PROTOTYPE begins losing letters.

First: the P and E at the ends dissolve. What remains lights up —

*\[ NOEME / rOTOTYP — the surviving letters pulse. The lit portion reads: noemE. \]*

Two more letters dissolve — R and O. What remains:

*\[ noeME \]*

Two more — T and Y:

*\[ noEME \]*

The final letter — O — begins moving toward the O already present in NOEME.

*\[ Two O's approach each other. For a single frame they touch — an infinity loop, two circles meeting. This is the visual proof of the show's hidden concept: two selves converging. ONE ME, written backwards*

As the last letter dissolves into the other O —

The eyes shift.

*\[ One pair of eyes goes pale grey. The other goes teal — pale mint green. The Condition, flickering through both faces simultaneously. The audience has no name for what they are seeing. They only feel that something is wrong. \]*

The remaining letters begin disappearing — NOEME — one at a time. But as each letter goes, what remains still makes sense read forward:

*\[ NOEME → N O E ME → N  EME → E ME → ME → E → \[gone\] \]*

As the final letter disappears — the eyes return to normal.

Both faces fade.

Black.

Silence.

*\[ The audience has just been told everything. They don't know it yet. \]*

— — —

*END TEASER*



# **BRIDGE IMAGES — What The Pilot Plants**

*These images appear in the pilot and will carry forward into later episodes. They are not meant to be explained. They are meant to be felt.


**Sky**

*Lucreta reaches for the sky through a window and is pulled away. Abby says they are not privileged enough to see the sky through city fog. The lake scene finally gives both characters — and the audience — sky. Each major Fragment will have a sky moment. Unity will make them connect.

**Window**

*Lucreta watches ordinary life through a window she cannot reach. The pilot ends with Abby receiving a life — a new home, a lake, openness — that Lucreta was denied. The hospital ending (later in the series) will echo both: a girl moving toward a window after surviving what should have ended her.

**Eye Color**

*Lucreta's pale mint eyes. The teaser's eye color shift. These are introduced as visual details. They will become evidence. The audience does not need to understand them yet.

**Red Hair**

*Lucreta has red hair. This is not incidental. It will bridge to later characters in ways that should feel like recognition when it arrives.

**Threshold / Door**

*Lucreta collapses before reaching the doorway. Phil and Abby walk through a doorway together at the episode's start. The episode is built around this inversion. Later acts will return to thresholds — crossed and refused — until the pattern becomes undeniable.

**The Condition**

*Never named in the pilot. Never explained. Visible only as Lucreta's fading eyes, her cough, her blood, her collapse — and as the brief eye-color shift in the teaser. The audience will form their own explanations. Those explanations will be right, and then they will be too small.


# **CHARACTER REFERENCE — Pilot**

## **Lucreta**

*Victorian-inspired world. Red hair. Pale mint-green eyes — already fading. Being prepared for a wedding she will never reach. She performs obedience and happiness while her body refuses to express either. She is not depressed. She is trapped in a ceremony that treats her function as more important than her personhood. She collapses before the threshold.

*She is the pilot's first voice. Her silence teaches the audience how to listen.

## **Vera**

*Lucreta's attendant. She cares genuinely — but her care is constrained by ritual. She hides her worry because she refuses to let Lucreta carry it. Her tear at the end of Scene 02 is real. She hides it so Lucreta doesn't see. She is not an obstacle. She is love under ceremony.

## **Philip / Phil**

*Futuristic city. The comedy of the episode. He uses humor the way some people use armor — not to hide vulnerability, but to keep the moment moving. He has been carrying a secret all day (the new home) and chosen jokes instead of explanation. He knows Abby well enough to read her silence without making her speak. His final line — 'This is our new home' — is plain. Not theatrical. That's why it lands.

## **Abigail / Abby**

*Phil's equal and the emotional center of Act 02. Sharp, warm, and unfooled. She feels the city more than Phil does — the compression, the fog, the missing sky, the suffering around her. She gives her necklace to a beggar because it is simply what she does. She cries at the lake not because it is beautiful but because it is open. The city had prepared her to expect walls. The lake is the opposite of a wall.


# **PRODUCTION NOTES**

## **On Withholding Faces**

*Both acts delay the face reveal. Lucreta is seen from behind before she appears in the mirror. Philip and Abigail are seen from behind, then through the outside city, before their faces turn. This is intentional and should be preserved in production. The face should be earned by the world first.

## **On Sound Design**

*Act 01 is nearly silent. The city in Act 02 is nearly overwhelming. This contrast is structural. The audience should feel the difference between Lucreta's world — controlled, ceremonially quiet — and Phil and Abby's world — compressed, pressured, alive. When the car doors close in Scene 07, the drop in ambient noise is a major emotional beat and should be designed as such.

## **On the Condition**

*The Condition is never named or explained in the pilot. It appears through Lucreta's eyes, her cough, her blood, and her collapse. It appears briefly in the teaser as a color shift. Production should resist any impulse to add explanatory dialogue or visual effects that tell the audience what they are seeing. The audience must feel that something is wrong before they understand what it is.

## **On the Teaser**

*The teaser is a typographic sequence with two overlaid faces. It requires careful sound design — the music hitting at the exact moment the image resolves is essential to the effect. The letter-dissolve sequence should feel inevitable in retrospect: the audience should be able to re-watch it after several episodes and understand that everything was there from the beginning.

## **On Tone**

*The pilot contains genuine warmth and genuine horror and asks neither to announce itself. Phil and Abby are funny. Lucreta is beautiful. The Condition does not care about either of these things. The audience should feel the warmth of Act 02 before they understand that what happened to Lucreta is part of the same story.
