A Chance Of Luck

INT. OLD CHAPEL — CONTINUOUS

Darkness behind a chapel bench.

One footstep.

Phil and Abby pass in front of it, seen first only by their feet.

The camera catches them already moving and follows low from behind.

As they cross the old chapel, the frame rises from their feet toward their backs.

Colored reflections move across the worn floorboards, interrupted by cheap lights hidden behind painted glass.

Plastic imitations of carved wood and fading paintings line the artificial sacred space around them.

By the doorway, their backs fill the frame. Their faces remain unseen.

Confetti suddenly explodes over them.

Fake applause crackles from hidden speakers, too cheerful for the mostly empty chapel.

Phil and Abby look at each other, startled.

Abby immediately starts brushing confetti from herself.

Phil opens the door with confetti still clinging to his hair and shoulders.

He puts on his sunglasses before brushing any of it off.

They step outside, the chapel’s fake celebration still stuck to them.

CUT TO:

EXT. CITY STREET — CONTINUOUS

The outside world enters before their faces.

The stench of the city greets them first. Then concrete walls fighting for every scrap of space, thinning the streets into little more than one-way passages. Rows of windows line the lower levels, each leaking a different smell of food into the walkway until they merge into something best experienced in passing.

Past them, the passages spill into roads lined with stalls pressed shoulder to shoulder, their owners fighting for the same space as the buildings looming above.

Metal grids and walkways crisscross overhead, venting steam into the gaps between buildings before it disappears into the haze. Whatever sunlight once reached the streets has long since been stolen by the structures above, or by the fog that devours everything the city once had to offer.

For a few seconds, the city holds: noise, movement, artificial lights, life, pressure.

Only then does the view shift to the front, revealing Phil and Abby.

Phil brushes confetti from his shoulder, sunglasses already in place.

PHIL

You know you have to deal with me for the rest of your life now, right?

ABBY

I wasn't aware this was a one-sided deal. Are you saying I've been duped?

MERCHANT (O.S.)

A gift for the missus?

The shout comes from the shade of a makeshift stall draped in flowers, squeezed between dozens of others competing for space. Phil turns his head toward the tall merchant without breaking stride.

PHIL

No need.

Oh, and the flowers?

She's already got me, thank you.

As he turns forward, his foot catches one of the bottles scattered beside a homeless man resting against the wall.

Abby turns her back to the city and looks toward the church doors.

ABBY

Is it too late to go back?

PHIL

You just watched me almost fall within a few steps and already want to go back? Am I supposed to survive this day?

A child darts through the crowd and bumps into her before disappearing between the stalls.

Phil turns back toward her, lowering his sunglasses.

PHIL

Karma here strikes fast, don't you think?

He follows it with a small wink before settling them back into place.

ABBY

Are you always this insufferable, darling?

He places a hand against his chest and tilts his chin upward.

PHIL

It's my middle name. My legacy. My most honed skill.

Shaking her head, she hooks her arm through his.

ABBY

The skills you ought to be focused on right now are the ones that'll make that wreck you call home actually livable.

Lowering his head, he peers over the rim of his sunglasses. He slows. She does not.

PHIL

Am I being duped?

She turns back, wind pulling hair across her face.

ABBY

Maybe.

The smile comes and goes, and she is already walking again.

His gaze drops to her dress. No pockets. The grin settles in.

PHIL

Don't tell me you've got a whole list tucked under your bra.

A trembling hand brushes against her skirt.

BEGGAR

Spare a coin?

A man in ragged clothes looks up from beside the wall, his hand still outstretched. She glances down at him.

ABBY

Could be a sign of good luck.

Phil rubs the back of his head, checks his pockets, and comes up empty.

PHIL

I think we're already cursed. You don't happen to have a bill stashed next to that imaginary list, do you?

ABBY

If I was trying to avoid bad luck, do you think I would've married you?

The beggar keeps his hand out. Sirens thread through traffic somewhere distant. The city offers nothing quieter than this.

Abby reaches up and unclasps the necklace from her neck, setting it into his palm. He stares at the weight of it.

A gentle nudge shifts her aside as a hand lands on the old man's shoulder.

PHIL

Looks like your day just turned around.

She turns back as the old man retreats into the shadows.

ABBY

See? Good fortune. Forever. Guaranteed.

Leaning slightly in:

ABBY (CONT'D)

And do you really need an imaginary list as an excuse to stare at my chest?

Phil starts to smirk, but it does not fully form as a tightening in the flow of bodies cuts across their path.

People and metal compress around them, forming a narrow passage before the flow opens again without stopping. They cross the street. He offers her his hand.

PHIL

Let's go. This isn't exactly what I pictured for our first afternoon.

ABBY

Really?

He leans toward her ear.

PHIL

Keep that up and I'm charging you for the ride.

Concrete forms in layered folds, one after another, stacking into distance until the horizon becomes a compressed wall of grey. Above it, jagged building edges cut into the sky as they move through the surge of bodies weaving in every direction.

ABBY

A quiet spot, huh? Bold claim, considering all this.

PHIL

You doubt me? Maybe you should, if that necklace hadn't bought us a ridiculous amount of luck. Must've been worth a fortune.

A sharp cry slices through the crowd's low roar. Her attention snaps to it. She drifts right, nearly colliding with someone. His hand closes around her arm before she goes far.

PHIL

Car's just ahead. Hasn't even been a day, and you're already making a run for it.

ABBY

What?

The noise swells. He raises his voice over it.

PHIL

I was expecting an... 'Are we there yet?'

ABBY

Are you calling me impatient?

PHIL

Me? You think I go around implying things about people?

ABBY

People?

PHIL

No. Just you.

Laughter breaks between them, light and out of place against the city's grinding pressure. He guides her through the last press of elbows and shoulders.

The car comes into view. She moves fast. Doors close in unison. The street noise drops to a muffle. They sit for a moment in sudden silence.

Hands on the wheel, he turns toward her.

PHIL

Ready for an adventure, my lady?

ABBY

Adventure. That's it? I expected a hotel, a good one. Maybe a spa. A proper dinner somewhere that doesn't make me want to cry.

His head dips with a faint sniff.

PHIL

Caught. Suppose it won't be a surprise anymore. You just married a man with one kidney, no liver, half a lung, and I'm still eight thousand and forty-two credits short.

She sets a hand on his shoulder, mock sadness in her tone.

ABBY

Poor man. So, since this isn't exactly what I pictured for our first afternoon married, what's left? Will it at least be magical?

He tries for serious and fails.

PHIL

The only magic you'll witness today is me, sugar puffs.

ABBY

Sugar puffs? Just drive. Get us out of here.

PHIL

Your wish, your command. Commander.

He maneuvers out of the parking spot, and her head drifts sideways, settling against the window.

Thousands of people move just beyond the glass. None of them look at her. None of them look at each other. She is right beside him, and he loses her anyway.

He glances over, just checking.

PHIL

Hungry?

She does not respond. He drives. Then his hand finds her leg.

PHIL

Hey... you okay?

Her head stays against the window. Her eyes drift from the road below, slowly lifting upward.

ABBY

Where do we go from here?

PHIL

From Narev we head south. Still a bit of a way. You can sleep if you want.

The fog sits against every window.

ABBY

We aren't even privileged enough to see the sky.

Her eyes do not move from the blur outside. He leans forward and looks up out of habit. Fog. Only fog. The city has never been without it. No one alive would know the difference.

He lets it go. Silence takes the stage.

An hour passes with her asleep. Daylight thins into something softer, and the city fills the gap, street-glow pressing back against the dark before it can settle. Night comes here but never completes itself.

ABBY

I'm hungry.

PHIL

How could you not be. You've been asleep for two days.

ABBY

Two days?

She squints at her cellphone screen.

ABBY (CONT'D)

Instead of jokes, why don't you sell your other liver, or whatever's left. Your brain, maybe. Get your wife some food.

She looks out the window to get her bearings.

ABBY (CONT'D)

How are we not there yet? Did you sleep too?

PHIL

You think? If I had, you'd be losing your mind right now. I didn't sleep, and here you are anyway.

ABBY

Find me food or I become your nightmare.

			PHILL

Your nightmare?

ABBY

I'm too hungry for this. Just food.

She waves him off.

PHILL

I was waiting to see if you'd work it out yourself.

ABBY

What?

PHILL

You already are.

Night takes them fully as they push along roads that no map bothers to name. The city's light refuses to die behind them, bleeding at the horizon. Even out here, the noise lives somewhere behind the ears.

She is on her phone, half-reclined, thumb moving on autopilot, when his voice cuts in beside her.

PHIL

Are—

She flinches hard, nearly dropping it.

PHIL (CONT'D)

—eeee yyoouuu readddyyy? Don't miss tomorrow, the fight between Jo—

ABBY

Stop!

PHIL

Yeah, I know. I hate long drives too.

The headlights eat the last stretch of dirt road. Beyond that, nothing.

PHIL

For a while now, you've been privileged. Didn't even notice.

Her thumb goes still. The phone tilts, slips, and lands in her lap untouched. She turns toward the window. In the glass, he catches the wet shine at the corner of her eye.

ABBY

It's... vast.

The city had left no room for anything beyond it.

The car rolls forward another few meters before slowing. The lurch rocks her sideways. She is already somewhere else.

Engine off. Lights off. The universe moves in to fill the gap.

PHIL

I know you won't enjoy what I'm about to ask.

ABBY

I don't care.

Too quick. Nothing is visible now but the scattered wreckage of light above them.

PHIL

I need you to cover your eyes.

ABBY

Okay. Covered.

She turns her head away.

ABBY (CONT'D)

You're being weird, by the way. No stupid comments in like forever. Hello?

Rustling. Close. She cannot place it. Then his mouth is right at her ear.

PHIL

I knew you were impatient.

The smack lands before the laugh does.

ABBY

You ass!

She drops into a mocking baritone.

ABBY (CONT'D)

So what's the big reveal? Oh, I'm sorry, I have to ask you this, blablabla—

PHIL

Can't you see?

ABBY

Can you?

PHIL

I'm naked.

Whatever she was about to say stalls completely.

ABBY

Are you—

A light flips on. A key sits in his palm. She does not know what it opens. It does not matter.

ABBY

If you've got handcuffs on the other hand, I'll strap you to that tree and leave you there. That one. Your tomb. Sleep well, my dearest.

He lets the smirk sit.

PHIL

This...

The pause is deliberate.

PHIL (CONT'D)

Abby. This is our freedom.

She keeps the pressure on.

ABBY

Oh. So we were, what, imprisoned? Enslaved?

Her face gives nothing. He can read straight through it anyway.

PHIL

I should've told you earlier. Given you more time with the view.

He closes his fingers around the key.

PHIL (CONT'D)

This is our new home.

END OF CHAPTER — NOEME
