Author: Diogo Oliveira

A BLURRED TRAGEDY

EXT. CITY STREETS — LUXURY DISTRICT — DAY

The walls separating the city from the luxury apartments no longer protect them from what the chaos has become.

Cars are parked in the middle of the road, alongside collapsed bodies, while everyone tries to figure out which direction to run.

PHIL'S cellphone hits the pavement.

One hand digs inside his pocket while the other widens it, as if that would help.

A couple pushes through the doors from inside, knocking PHIL back as they rush past without looking up. One of her bags drops to the floor.

She keeps going.

INT. PHIL'S APARTMENT BUILDING — LOBBY — CONTINUOUS

The elevator is stuck, neighbors loading everything they can into it.

Trying to reach his floor, PHIL keeps bumping his glasses as he presses the keys against his face, rushing to get home.

When PHIL lowers his head to fit the key into the lock, a knock from his foot slides the door open.

PHIL pushes it against the wall.

PHIL

ABBY!

A glance toward the kitchen, then down the hall.

PHIL (CONT'D)

ABBY! Come on—we gotta move now!

INT. PHIL'S APARTMENT — STAIRS / HALLWAY — CONTINUOUS

PHIL turns right toward the stairs, only to fumble on the first step. His hands catch the stairs before his feet do.

PHIL reaches the top with his shoulder running against the wall.

SERA (O.S.)

Daddy!

PHIL straightens his glasses in a fast stride toward the room.

Using his elbow to open the door—

PHIL

Sera! Where is Mommy?

INT. PHIL'S APARTMENT — SERA'S ROOM — CONTINUOUS

SERA

Mommyyy!

SERA keeps waving the plushy until it hits him.

A folded piece of paper slips out.

PHIL sees it as it hits the carpet.

SERA'S voice goes muffled.

SERA

D…y!

PHIL reaches for it.

For a moment, there are two.

Then one.

His hand closes around the paper.

His legs give out.

PHIL misses the bed and hits the floor.

SERA

…y!

The plushy lands against his foot.

PHIL looks up.

Nothing reaches him.

And he opens it.

Smudged letters gradually shape into words.

ON THE LETTER

"Phil…"

His breathing destabilizes the letter.
