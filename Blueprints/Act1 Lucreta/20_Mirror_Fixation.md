# Scene — Mirror Fixation

Status:
- Scene blueprint
- Prose exists
- Adaptation/script version not written

Act:
- Act 01 — Lucreta

Characters:
- Lucreta
- Vera
- Off-screen family voice / wedding pressure

Location:
- Lucreta's bedroom / mirror area
- Door to ceremony

Purpose:
- Continue directly from the delayed face reveal in the previous scene.
- Turn Lucreta's pale-mint eyes into a clue through behavior, not exposition.
- Show Lucreta's awareness that something is wrong before the scene explains anything.
- Show Vera trying to rescue the wedding moment from the condition while hiding her own worry.
- Establish the illness-performance pattern later echoed by Erit.

Scene Summary:
Lucreta closes her eyes before the mirror. A knock and impatient voice from outside break the silence, pressing the wedding back into the room. When Lucreta opens her eyes again, her pale-mint reflection anchors her attention and erases everything else.

Vera tries to break the silence and pull Lucreta out of the fixed gaze. She teases about fear of womanhood and the marriage burdening her, not simply to pressure Lucreta, but to restore the room and rescue the moment from the condition taking it over. Lucreta replies with the words expected of her, but physically almost nothing changes.

A cough tears through Lucreta's answer. She catches herself against the table and struggles to breathe, then forces the expected line to completion. The cough returns sharper. Blood appears. Lucreta wipes it away and instinctively turns the blood into the gown before fully remembering what she is wearing.

Vera's hands still. For a brief moment, worry appears across her reflection. Then a practiced calm replaces it. Vera tries to preserve the moment by asking about the future child. Lucreta answers coldly through the reflection, exposing the way everyone has prepared her for function more than personhood. A second knock breaks the fragile exposure. Vera touches Lucreta's shoulder and arm, tells her to go, and hides her own tear from the reflection.

Emotional Function:
- Start emotion: fixation and private dread.
- Shift: the body breaks the fixation, and Vera's performance of normality becomes visible.
- End emotion: sadness hidden under ceremony as Lucreta is pushed toward the wedding.
- Reader/viewer should feel: unease, denial, dread, and the cruelty of pretending the future can continue.

Character Function:
- Lucreta:
  - Shows awareness, restraint, and dread.
  - Her fixation on her eyes suggests she knows something is wrong before anyone says it.
  - Her words perform obedience and happiness while her body refuses to express either.
- Vera:
  - Tries to pull Lucreta out of the fixation so the condition does not consume the wedding moment.
  - Cares for Lucreta, but hides worry and sadness quickly to preserve the ritual.
- Off-screen family voice:
  - Represents ceremony pressure, impatience, and social obligation from outside the room.

Structural Function:
- Begins directly from the delayed face reveal in the previous scene.
- Turns Lucreta's eyes into a behavioral clue.
- Establishes the condition before the reader has language for it.
- Creates an early example of illness hidden beneath social ritual.
- Echoes later Erit scenes where family/status preparation continues while symptoms worsen.

Performance Rule:
- Lucreta's replies perform obedience and happiness, but physically almost nothing changes.
- Her face remains cold, controlled, and nearly emotionless through the mirror exchange.
- The sarcasm comes from the mismatch between what she says and what her body refuses to express.
- Do not play the lines as sincere joy.
- Do not play the coldness as hatred toward Vera.
- Vera is different: she feels, worries, and grieves, but hides it to preserve the ceremony and protect the moment.

Action Flow:

F01 — Lucreta closes her eyes before the mirror.

F02 — The first knock breaks the silence from outside.

F03 — Lucreta opens her eyes and fixates on the pale-mint reflection.

F04 — Vera tries to break the silence and pull Lucreta out of the fixation.

F05 — Lucreta replies without leaving the mirror fixation.

F06 — The cough breaks Lucreta's words and body control.

F07 — Lucreta regains enough breath to finish the expected answer.

F08 — The second cough returns sharper and blood appears.

F09 — Lucreta wipes the blood away and instinctively turns it into the gown.

F10 — Lucreta realizes what she has done.

F11 — Vera's hands still.

F12 — Vera's worry surfaces in reflection, then is hidden behind a practiced smile.

F13 — Vera asks about the future child.

F14 — Lucreta answers with a cold cut through the reflection.

F15 — Vera's smile weakens and the room loses warmth.

F16 — The second knock bursts through the moment.

F17 — Vera touches Lucreta's shoulder and arm before stepping away.

F18 — Vera tells Lucreta to go.

F19 — Vera's tear slips down and she hides it from reflection.

Dialogue / Cue Attachments:

F02:
- Dialogue:
  - Off-screen voice: “Lucreta! Are you ready yet? The guests have been waiting nearly an hour already. Surely you do not intend to leave your groom languishing from boredom before he can even marry you.”
- Cue:
  - The voice carries impatience.
  - This is outside ceremony pressure, not Vera's kind of care.

F03:
- Cue:
  - Her pale-mint eyes immediately anchor the reflection.
  - The rest of her comes into focus around them only slowly.
  - The eyes should feel wrong through her fixation, before any explanation.

F04:
- Dialogue:
  - Vera: “Do not tell me you have grown fearful of womanhood at the final hour, dear. You linger so long one might think this marriage burdens you terribly.”
- Cue:
  - Vera is trying to break the silence.
  - She is trying to pull Lucreta back into the room and out of the fixed gaze at her reflection.
  - Vera understands that the fixation is ruining the moment for Lucreta by bringing the condition over the wedding.
  - The line should feel like an attempt to restore normality, not simply pressure.

F05:
- Dialogue:
  - Lucreta: “No, Vera. Quite the contrary.”
  - Lucreta: “Nothing could—”
- Cue:
  - Lucreta remains fixed on the mirror.
  - A faint smile may touch her lips, but physically almost nothing changes.
  - The body does not warm with the words.

F06:
- Cue:
  - The words break under the spasm in her chest.
  - Lucreta turns sharply.
  - One hand catches the edge of the table.
  - For a moment she cannot breathe.

F07:
- Dialogue:
  - Lucreta: “Nothing could mar this day.”
  - Lucreta: “There is nothing I have ever desired more than this hour.”
- Cue:
  - She draws in a slow, careful breath before finishing.
  - The answer performs expected happiness.
  - The delivery is quiet and controlled, not sincere joy.

F08:
- Cue:
  - The cough returns before the final word has fully left her mouth.
  - It is sharper this time.
  - A faint stain blooms against her lips.

F09:
- Cue:
  - Her eyes widen.
  - Her hand rises quickly to her mouth.
  - She wipes the blood away before turning her palm against the fabric of the gown.
  - The movement is instinctive, as though the blood might disappear there.

F10:
- Cue:
  - She only then remembers what she is wearing.
  - The shock lands because the reaction happened without thought.

F11:
- Cue:
  - Vera's hands still.
  - This is the first clear sign that Vera has seen the severity of the moment.

F12:
- Cue:
  - Worry surfaces across Vera's reflection only briefly.
  - A practiced calm settles over it instead.
  - The careful smile is gentle enough to preserve the moment rather than shatter it.
  - Vera is not emotionless; she hides worry so Lucreta does not have to carry it.

F13:
- Dialogue:
  - Vera: “So—”
  - Vera: “How long before I may tend to the child you are certain to bless us with?”
- Cue:
  - A brush slides through Lucreta's hair.
  - Vera uses the future child to preserve the moment and move past the blood.
  - The smile is careful, not fully natural.

F14:
- Dialogue:
  - Lucreta: “And yet you speak of that child so readily.”
  - Lucreta: “As though you have spent longer preparing for its coming than my own mother ever spent preparing for me.”
- Cue:
  - Lucreta's chin tilts subtly.
  - She glances toward Vera.
  - This is cold, precise, and controlled.
  - It is not random cruelty; it exposes how everyone has prepared her for function more than personhood.

F15:
- Cue:
  - Vera's smile weakens briefly.
  - Much of the room's fragile warmth goes with it.
  - Lucreta watches her.

F16:
- Dialogue:
  - Off-screen voice: “Lucreta! Your father will grow tired of the groom before you are even married. Come out, girl—at this rate you shall emerge old and wrinkled before the ceremony begins.”
- Cue:
  - The second knock is harsher and more intrusive.
  - It breaks the fragile emotional exposure between Lucreta and Vera.

F17:
- Cue:
  - Vera slides a hand across Lucreta's shoulder and down her arm.
  - She steps away.
  - The touch is care constrained by ritual.

F18:
- Dialogue:
  - Vera: “Go, dear. No one deserves this moment more than you.”
- Cue:
  - The line is genuine encouragement, but also part of keeping the ceremony alive.
  - Vera has already hidden most of her sadness by the time she says it.

F19:
- Cue:
  - A tear slips down Vera's cheek.
  - She wipes it away before it can fall any further.
  - She turns slightly, hiding her reflection from Lucreta's view.
  - Vera's sadness is real, but she refuses to let it become another thing Lucreta has to carry.

Important Notes:
- The first knock should not pull Lucreta away from her eyes. She remains fixated.
- Vera does not notice the reflection early. Vera's worry appears after the cough/blood moment.
- The cough and blood break the performance before the room can pretend again.
- Lucreta must remain a person first, not only a clue.
