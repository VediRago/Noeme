## Act Feeling

Lucreta carries the shape of a wedding, but not the feeling of one.

It should feel joyful, yet the atmosphere remains heavy.

Everyone around Lucreta knows she is sick. Lucreta knows it too.

They keep playing along to make the moment easier for her.

It does the opposite.

It creates an aura of fakeness.

Her reflection is the only thing in the room not lying to her.

## Act Purpose

Act 01 establishes the emotional language of NOEME.

The Condition is introduced through observation rather than explanation. The audience is never told what is wrong. They are left with fragments: a cold bride, worried glances, pale-mint eyes, a cough, and a collapse.

Preserve the doorway contrast.

Lucreta collapses before crossing the threshold of her wedding.

Act 02 begins with Philip and Abigail crossing theirs.

## Setting & Atmosphere

Lucreta takes place inside a Victorian-inspired aristocratic society.

The act should feel formal, controlled, and ceremonial. Appearance, etiquette, family, and social duty matter.

## Important Motifs

**Pale-Mint Eyes**  
The eyes are the most visible sign of the Condition. They pull Lucreta back toward the truth everyone else is avoiding.

**Red Hair**  
Lucreta's red hair establishes an important visual bridge carried by later fragments.

**Window**  
The window represents life continuing beyond the room.

**Reflection**  
Her reflection is the only thing in the room not lying to her.

**Threshold**  
The wedding doorway becomes the life Lucreta cannot reach.

## Scene Structure

### 10 — Bedroom Preparation

Introduces the wedding preparation, the withheld face, the window, and the pull toward ordinary life outside.

### 20 — Mirror Fixation

Centers the act on Lucreta's reflection, pale-mint eyes, Vera's concern, and the future everyone keeps pretending is still available.

### 30 — Wedding Collapse

Completes the failed threshold as Lucreta tries to continue toward the ceremony and collapses before crossing the doorway.
