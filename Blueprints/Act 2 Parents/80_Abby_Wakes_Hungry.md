# Scene — Abby Wakes Hungry

Status:
- Scene blueprint
- Prose exists
- Adaptation/script version not written

Act:
- Act 02 — Parents

Part:
- Part 01 — Marriage To Lake

Source:
- Parents Part 1 prose

Characters:
- Philip / Phil
- Abigail / Abby

Location:
- Moving car
- Road away from the city
- Route toward the lake

Purpose:
- Break the quiet breathing passage of 70_Time_Passage_Toward_Lake.
- Bring Abby back into the scene through an ordinary human need instead of another reflective beat.
- Restore Phil and Abby's playful rhythm after silence and sleep.
- Continue the long drive toward the lake without revealing the destination or the key.

Emotional Function:
- Start emotion: quiet after sleep and time passing.
- Shift: Abby wakes and pulls the scene back into lively human rhythm.
- End emotion: playful irritation, hunger, and renewed couple energy.
- Reader/viewer should feel: life returning after the breathing scene.

Character Function:
- Phil:
  - Responds instantly with humor.
  - Keeps the drive moving while letting Abby re-enter the rhythm on her own terms.
  - Hides the destination and the key without seeming secretive.
- Abby:
  - Wakes with hunger before deeper emotion.
  - Returns to banter quickly.
  - Uses mock threats and impatience to reassert herself after sleeping through the drive.

Structural Function:
- Follows 70_Time_Passage_Toward_Lake.
- Replaces reflection with appetite and comedy.
- Keeps the lake reveal delayed.
- Creates a human pacing reset before the night-road approach and sky reveal.
- Prepares the next movement where the road becomes unnamed, darker, and farther from the city.

World / Scene Texture:
- The city should now feel farther away but not completely gone.
- Daylight has thinned into softer light.
- City glow still presses against the dark behind them.
- The road feels longer than Abby expected.

Visual / Camera Function:
- Begin with Abby waking from the window.
- Let the audience feel she has been asleep for real time.
- Use her phone check and look outside to show disorientation.
- Keep Phil mostly on the road, glancing only when needed.

## Action Flow

F01 — Abby wakes from sleep against the window.

F02 — Abby stretches toward Phil.

F03 — Abby announces that she is hungry.

F04 — Phil immediately jokes about how long she has been asleep.

F05 — Abby reacts to the exaggeration and checks her phone.

F06 — Abby shifts from confusion into complaint.

F07 — Abby looks outside to get her bearings.

F08 — Abby notices they are still not there.

F09 — Abby asks whether Phil slept too.

F10 — Phil answers quickly and turns the question back on her.

F11 — Abby demands food.

F12 — Phil starts to repeat her threat back to her.

F13 — Abby cuts him off because she is too hungry for the bit.

F14 — Abby says she was waiting for him to work something out himself.

F15 — Phil asks what she means.

F16 — Abby tells him he already is her nightmare.

F17 — The scene carries into the darker, unnamed road ahead.

## Dialogue / Cue Attachments

F01:
- Cue:
  - Abby should wake naturally, not dramatically.
  - The audience should feel the previous scene's time passage has been real.

F02:
- Cue:
  - Abby stretches her arms toward Phil.
  - Her body language should feel sleepy and familiar.

F03:
- Dialogue:
  - Abby: “I'm hungry.”
- Cue:
  - The line should be simple and immediate.
  - This is the clean break from the breathing scene.

F04:
- Dialogue:
  - Phil: “How could you not be.”
  - Phil: “You've been asleep for two days.”
- Cue:
  - Phil looks at her briefly.
  - The joke should land fast and naturally, not as a big performance.

F05:
- Dialogue:
  - Abby: “Two days?”
- Cue:
  - Abby squints at her phone screen.
  - She knows he is joking, but checks anyway because she is still waking.

F06:
- Dialogue:
  - Abby: “Instead of jokes, why don't you sell your other liver, or whatever's left. Your brain, maybe. Get your wife some food.”
- Cue:
  - Abby moves from sleepy confusion into playful complaint.
  - The line should feel affectionate and irritated at the same time.

F07:
- Cue:
  - Abby looks out the window to get her bearings.
  - The outside should confirm that time has passed and the city is farther behind.

F08:
- Dialogue:
  - Abby: “How are we not there yet? Did you sleep too?”
- Cue:
  - The question is practical frustration, not fear.
  - She is awake now and immediately pushing back into the rhythm.

F10:
- Dialogue:
  - Phil: “You think?”
  - Phil: “If I had, you'd be losing your mind right now. I didn't sleep, and here you are anyway.”
- Cue:
  - The answer should be fast and ready.
  - Phil has the line before she can take control of the joke.

F11:
- Dialogue:
  - Abby: “Find me food or I become your nightmare.”
- Cue:
  - Abby should sound serious only in the way hungry people pretend to be serious.

F12:
- Dialogue:
  - Phil: “Your nightmare?”
- Cue:
  - Phil turns his head slightly toward her.
  - He is about to play with the phrase.

F13:
- Dialogue:
  - Abby: “I'm too hungry for this. Just food.”
- Cue:
  - Abby waves him off.
  - She refuses to let him stretch the bit because hunger has priority.

F14:
- Dialogue:
  - Abby: “I was waiting to see if you'd work it out yourself.”
- Cue:
  - She keeps the upper hand by pretending she set the trap.

F15:
- Dialogue:
  - Phil: “What?”
- Cue:
  - Phil's eyes stay forward.
  - He should sound wary because he knows she is setting up the turn.

F16:
- Dialogue:
  - Abby: “You already are.”
- Cue:
  - This completes the nightmare joke.
  - Let it land lightly before the scene moves back into travel.

F17:
- Cue:
  - Night takes them more fully after the exchange.
  - The next scene should push them along roads that no map bothers to name.

- Keep the rhythm playful and human.
- Do not let the exchange drag too long.

Important Notes:
- Do not reopen Abby's earlier existential uncertainty.
- Do not mention the key.
- Do not reveal the lake yet.
- This scene works because hunger interrupts reflection.
- The comedy should feel like Phil and Abby returning to their normal operating language.
- Keep the movement grounded in the long drive.

Scene Summary:
After the quiet passage of travel, Abby wakes against the window and immediately announces that she is hungry. Phil answers with a quick joke that she has been asleep for two days. Still waking, Abby checks her phone, complains, and tells him to sell whatever organs or brain he has left to get his wife some food. She looks outside, realizes they still have not arrived, and asks whether he slept too. Phil fires back that if he had slept she would be losing her mind already, and yet she is doing that anyway. Abby threatens to become his nightmare if he does not find food. Phil tries to play with the phrase, but Abby cuts him off because she is too hungry. She then turns the joke back on him: she was waiting to see if he would work it out himself, because he already is her nightmare. The couple's playful rhythm has returned, and the drive continues toward the darker road ahead.
