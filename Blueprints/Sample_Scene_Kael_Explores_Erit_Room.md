# Scene — Kael Explores Erit's Room

Status:
- Scene blueprint
- Prose not written

Act:
- Act 08 — Erit

Characters:
- Kael
- Erit

Location:
- Erit's house
- Erit's room
- Mirror / dressing area

Purpose:
- First major Kael / Erit bonding moment.
- First time Kael smiles.
- First time Erit forgets the disease for a moment.
- Establish Kael and Erit as reflections rather than simple romantic tension.
- Show Kael touching Erit's world before she understands Erit.
- Show Erit choosing tenderness instead of anger.

Scene Summary:
Kael wanders through Erit's house before properly knowing her. She absorbs the unfamiliar beauty of the place, pauses at Erit's paintings, then finds Erit's room filled with jewelry, makeup, dresses, and personal objects. Drawn to a world of femininity, identity, performance, and expression, Kael tries on a necklace in front of the mirror and may awkwardly touch the makeup.

Erit enters quickly after crying and finds the girl her parents spoke about, the girl who might be an answer for her, standing in her room trying her things. Kael freezes, shocked and exposed, and tries to explain but cannot speak. Instead of becoming angry, Erit wipes her tears, understands the moment, and chooses to join it. She says, "No, base first," then steps closer and begins helping Kael. The scene becomes a shared ritual. Kael smiles for the first time, and Erit sees it in the mirror before smiling too.

Emotional Function:
- Start emotion: Kael as an outsider moving through a house that does not belong to her.
- Shift: Erit enters from grief and finds Kael awkwardly trying to become part of her world.
- End emotion: Both girls share a small moment of joy through the mirror.
- Reader should feel: tenderness, surprise, intimacy, relief, and the sense that both girls briefly become free from what defines them.

Character Function:
- Kael: Shows curiosity, shame, wonder, and the first visible opening into joy.
- Erit: Shows that she is not only sick, privileged, or protected; she can choose tenderness, playfulness, and connection.

Structural Function:
- Supports the Mirror Bridge and the Kael / Erit reflection relationship.
- Plants the idea that Kael and Erit are drawn to each other through self-recognition, not simple romance.
- Gives the later tragedy more emotional weight by letting them share a private, ordinary moment first.
- Shows Kael entering Erit's interior life before understanding her condition or family role.

Important Notes:
- The mirror is central. It should not feel decorative.
- The audience may initially read the intimacy as romantic tension, but the deeper truth is self-recognition.
- Kael sees a life she never had: beauty, status, objects, room, family, identity.
- Erit sees someone who does not treat her as a dying girl.
- Kael enters through wonder, not pity.
- Erit chooses tenderness over possession.
- The scene should not explain the condition.
- The disease should be temporarily absent from the emotional center.
- The scene works because it lets both girls become girls for a moment.
