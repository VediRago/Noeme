# Scene — Valkare Receives the Research Failure Report

Status: scene blueprint / prose to be written

Act: Act 08 — Erit

## Purpose

- Introduce Valkare through power, restraint, and pressure.
- Show the elite political world around the Devaar family.
- Connect Ventari house status, gladiator spectacle, and scientific ambition.
- Establish that the research is already producing horrific human cost.
- Show Valkare's anger as grief and desperation, not simple cruelty.
- Prepare the emotional context for Erit's condition and the family's later moral collapse.

## Core Emotional Function

Valkare begins the scene as a controlled elite figure speaking politics and reputation.

The report turns the room from status into failure.

His anger is not directed at the messenger. It is directed at the process, the cost, and the fact that none of it is saving Erit fast enough.

## Setting

A high room belonging to Valkare or the Devaar estate.

Visual anchors:

- Large desk.
- Papers and research reports.
- Glass of water.
- Great glass pane / window facing clouds and sky.
- Monitor used for political communication.
- Elite Roman-inspired future aesthetic.
- Valkare dressed formally, controlled, ceremonial, powerful.

## Characters Present

- Valkare Devaar.
- A senator speaking through a monitor.
- A messenger / researcher / aide. Name to be inserted later.

## Scene Beats

1. Valkare speaks through a monitor with a senator.
2. The senator praises or discusses the latest gladiator battle.
3. The battle has put the other houses to shame, especially Ventari house.
4. The conversation shows Valkare's elite position and the political value of spectacle.
5. Valkare is interrupted by an aide / researcher.
6. The interruption concerns the latest research development.
7. Valkare ends or pauses the senator conversation.
8. The aide reports the results of the eye enhancement subjects.
9. Nineteen subjects were enlisted.
10. Seven died.
11. Twelve lost their vision.
12. No successful recovery / useful result.
13. Valkare becomes furious.
14. His anger is not aimed at the person delivering the report.
15. He knocks papers and the glass of water from the table.
16. He plants both hands on the desk.
17. He asks about the other research.
18. The aide says there is no news yet.
19. Preserving bodies is not something they have fully delved into.
20. It may take months, possibly years.
21. Valkare turns away from the desk.
22. He faces the great glass pane and the clouds beyond it.
23. He regains control.
24. He thanks the aide for the news.
25. He says that if anything else arises, they are to come to him right away.

## Important Emotional Notes

- Valkare should not feel like a cartoon villain.
- He is powerful, but the power is failing him.
- The deaths and blindness should land as horror, not just statistics.
- He is angry because the research is brutal and still not enough.
- The contrast matters: political praise on the monitor, then human cost in the room.
- The window/clouds should echo longing, distance, and the larger sky motif.

## Possible Dialogue Fragments

Senator:

> The last gladiator battle put the other houses to shame.

Aide:

> Of the nineteen subjects enlisted for the eye enhancement procedure... seven died. The remaining twelve lost their vision.

Valkare:

> And the other research?

Aide:

> No news yet, your highness. The preservation process is not something we have delved into before. It may take months. Years, perhaps.

Valkare:

> Thank you for the news.

> If anything else arises, come to me right away.

## Why This Scene Matters

This scene establishes that Erit's family is not simply wealthy and cruel.

They are operating inside a world where status, spectacle, science, desperation, and family love are tangled together.

Valkare's first impression should be controlled power cracking under private failure.

## Later Payoff Potential

- Makes Valkare's care for Erit more believable.
- Makes Fedra's later moral crossing feel like part of a family pattern under pressure.
- Establishes the cost of the research before Kael/Erit relationship softens the act.
- Creates contrast with later intimate scenes: public power versus private helplessness.
