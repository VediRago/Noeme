# NOEME — Narrative Design Portfolio Overview

NOEME is presented here as a narrative design and story direction portfolio.

The purpose of this repository is not to expose every private development note. It is to demonstrate that I can write, structure, and direct a long-form story with emotional continuity, visual logic, recurring motifs, and a controlled reveal system.

## What This Portfolio Demonstrates

NOEME is built around separated lives that appear disconnected before the audience begins recognizing shared images, symptoms, gestures, and emotional consequences between them. The project is designed to show control over:

- serialized story structure
- character-driven tragedy and emotional escalation
- recurring visual motifs
- layered reveals
- scene-to-scene continuity
- cinematic pacing
- script formatting and dialogue
- narrative systems that can support interactive or game-adjacent storytelling

The work is meant to show more than premise. It shows how a story can be directed across prose, scripts, blueprints, teaser material, and structural design documents.

## Game Narrative Relevance

For game development, NOEME demonstrates the kind of narrative thinking needed to hold a player or audience through mystery, delayed information, emotional payoff, and systemic structure.

The project uses repetition, contrast, visual anchors, and delayed recognition as design tools. Important story information is not delivered only through exposition. It is built through pattern, image, rhythm, and consequence.

That matters for games because narrative often has to survive outside a single linear scene. It has to work through environment, repeated symbols, character memory, fragmented information, and player interpretation.

## My Role

This repository presents my work as writer, narrative designer, and story director.

I developed the premise, characters, structural rules, episode logic, screenplay material, prose material, teaser direction, and the larger narrative architecture behind the project.
