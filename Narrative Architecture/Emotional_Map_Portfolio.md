# Emotional Map

This file tracks what each act is designed to make the reader feel.

It does not summarize plot. It maps breathing room, pressure, rupture, overload, and stillness across the story.

---

## Emotional Rhythm

**Breathing room** — Space to settle into character and relationship before pressure rises.

**Pressure** — Tension narrowing toward danger, loss, or collapse.

**Rupture** — The point where the act's emotional rhythm breaks.

**Overload** — Tension beyond normal scene rhythm or stable perspective.

**Stillness** — Aftermath. Quiet space where the reader can feel what changed.

---

## Act Structure

### Act 01 — Lucreta
Brief breathing room becomes pressure quickly. Beauty and possibility are closed around by unease and inevitability. Establishes the first pattern: a life reaching toward continuation, then ending before it can begin.

**Feeling:** Fragile beauty, unease, restraint, inevitability.

---

### Act 02 — Phil & Abigail
Long breathing room — humor, routine, love, family — before slow pressure and irreversible loss. The impact hits harder because the reader has time to care first.

**Feeling:** Warmth, attachment, ordinary happiness, then loss.

---

### Act 03 — Kael Arrival
A somber break. Kael enters quiet, displaced, strange, and lonely. The tonal reset allows the reader to feel that the world has changed without yet knowing how pieces connect.

**Feeling:** Stillness, alienation, curiosity, displacement.

---

### Act 04 — Sera Alone
Low pressure beneath survival. Sera continues moving inside an unsafe world. Guarded humor and surface stability mask deeper instability.

**Feeling:** Survival, loneliness, guarded humor, hidden tension.

---

### Act 05 — Erit & Devaar Family
Pressure from the start. Erit shows symptoms. The family is already crossing moral lines. Controlled on the surface, increasingly desperate underneath.

**Feeling:** Refinement, illness, desperation, moral compromise.

---

### Act 06 — Kael & Sera
Breathing room with a late rupture. Kael and Sera's relationship creates curiosity, trust, humor, and fragile safety. Tension spikes only near the end.

**Feeling:** Curiosity, guarded trust, humor, tenderness, then sudden danger.

**Core Bridge — The Apartment:**

Sera finds the abandoned apartment before Kael. The bright sky enters through broken walls because it marks Sera's transition from isolation into possibility. The space becomes her first self-made threshold between loneliness and attachment.

Later, Sera brings Kael into the apartment. She teaches reading and writing. They build a daily rhythm. When Kael's condition worsens, Sera tries to get medicine — and triggers the signal that brings the Devaars.

By the time the Devaars arrive, Kael is too weak to resist. Fedra hugs Sera in recognition: *Sera became home before anyone rescued them.*

This structural moment — Sera's choice to shelter another child — makes the apartment more than a hiding place. It becomes the emotional anchor that later justifies why the tragedy matters.

---

### Act 07 — Convergence
Tension all the way. Characters operate from incomplete information. The condition keeps narrowing available choices. The reader should feel walls closing before full tragedy hits.

**Feeling:** Suspicion, dread, confusion, urgency, inevitability.

---

### Act 08 — Erit Body Found
Rupture. Finding Erit's body breaks the emotional structure and the reader's sense of safety. Incomplete information becomes irreversible emotional certainty.

**Feeling:** Horror, betrayal, grief, disbelief.

---

### Act 09 — Unity
Overload. Not ordinary tension. Grief, perception, and identity can no longer remain localized. The act begins from devastation, then accelerates into unstable images, voices, and sensory fragments that turn the hidden structure into direct experience.

**Feeling:** Grief, disorientation, recognition, overwhelming continuation.

---

### Act 10 — Hospital Ending
Stillness after overload. Small, clinical, ordinary, strangely impossible. Not explanation. Evidence that rules changed.

**Feeling:** Quiet, disbelief, fragile hope, grey ambiguity.
