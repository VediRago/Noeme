# Narrative Architecture

This folder explains how NOEME works as a narrative system. It should remain readable to someone who has not seen the full workshop notes.

NOEME is built from character stories first. Lucreta, Sera, Kael, Erit, Phil, Abby, and others must matter as people before their lives become evidence of a larger pattern. The deeper structure is not meant to replace the emotional story. It exists so the connections feel inevitable instead of random.

## Layers

NOEME works through several layers at once. The surface layer is the visible emotional story: love, illness, grief, survival, guilt, and attachment. Beneath that is the structural layer: recurring images, symptoms, outcomes, doors, windows, mirrors, eye color, sky, water, and displacement. Beneath that is the interpretation layer, where the audience forms working explanations. The deepest layer is the hidden system that makes the connections coherent.

The audience does not need the blueprint while watching. They only need to feel that the story has a reason behind its echoes.

## Bridges

A bridge is a connection between two moments that becomes meaningful later. It can be an image, line, gesture, symptom, object, or emotional rhythm. A bridge should not exist only as a clue. It should first work inside the immediate scene, then gain additional weight when another moment echoes it.

Lucreta's window and the hospital girl's window are a bridge. Lucreta is denied the future. The hospital girl survives long enough to move toward it. Same gesture. Different outcome.

## Stretching

Stretching is the method by which NOEME lets the audience form valid explanations, then later expands those explanations without invalidating them. The goal is not to trick the viewer. The goal is to let understanding grow.

A weaker mystery says: you were wrong. NOEME should say: you were right to notice that, but there is more.

The virus, the Condition, eye color, displacement, and Unity should all work this way. Early interpretations remain useful even when the story grows beyond them.

## Design Rule

Every major scene should be at least one of two things: a treasure, a key, or both. A treasure is satisfying on its own. A key later opens another layer of meaning. Lucreta's story must matter as Lucreta's life before it becomes evidence of the Condition or Unity.

The ideal reaction is not simply "I know the answer." It is closer to: "I knew there was a reason, and now the earlier moments feel larger."
