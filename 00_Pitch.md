# NOEME — Pitch

NOEME is a story of contradiction: how can everything be connected if it only exists through separation?

Within a system that enforces separation, the self fractures into separate lives, each carrying a different outcome of tragedy, hope, love, guilt, and survival. Lucreta opens the door to Phil and Abby, Sera's parents, before Sera is even born. Her red hair and green eyes echo Lucreta's, forming the first visible step toward a vast spectrum of inherited feeling — ending in a tragedy that breaks the very system that caused this self to die so young.

NOEME follows these separate lives as human stories first. A bride collapses before reaching her wedding. A newly married couple escapes a suffocating city and discovers a home beneath an impossible sky. A child not yet born becomes the emotional consequence of both. Their worlds appear separate, but their images, symptoms, choices, and losses begin to echo across distance.

The series is not built around immediate explanation. It is built around recognition. The audience should feel the connection before they can define it.

## Format

Serialized drama series.

## Tone

Quiet dread beneath warmth. The horror does not announce itself. Symptoms look biological. Tragedy looks like bad luck. Hope arrives through ordinary human gestures before the larger structure becomes visible.

## Core Question

How can everything be connected if connection can only exist through separation?
