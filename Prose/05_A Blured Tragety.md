The walls separating the city from the luxury apartments no longer protect them from what the chaos has become. Cars are parked in the middle of the road, alongside collapsed bodies, while everyone tries to figure out which direction to run.

His cellphone hits the pavement.

One hand digs inside his pocket while the other widens it, as if that would help.

A couple pushes through the doors from inside, knocking him back as they rush past without looking up. One of her bags drops to the floor.

She keeps going.

The elevator is stuck, neighbors loading everything they can into it.

Trying to reach his floor, Phil keeps bumping his glasses as he presses the keys against his face, rushing to get home. When he lowers his head to fit the key into the lock, a knock from his foot slides the door open.

He pushes it against the wall.

“ABBY!”

A glance toward the kitchen, then down the hall.

“ABBY! Come on—we gotta move now!”

He turns right toward the stairs, only to fumble on the first step. His hands catch the stairs before his feet do. He reaches the top with his shoulder running against the wall.

“Daddy!”

His hand straightens his glasses in a fast stride toward the room.

Using his elbow to open the door—

“Sera! Where is Mommy?”

“Mommyyy!”

Sera keeps waving the plushy until it hits him.

A folded piece of paper slips out.

Phil sees it as it hits the carpet.

Sera’s voice goes muffled.

“D…y!”

He reaches for it.

For a moment, there are two.

Then one.

His hand closes around the paper.

His legs give out.

He misses the bed and hits the floor.

“…y!”

The plushy lands against his foot.

He looks up.

Nothing reaches him.

And he opens it.

Smudged letters gradually shape into words.

“Phil…”

His breathing destabilizes the letter.

“…y!”

“I don’t have to tell you how hard this is. It’s impossible, and you know…”

His head falls back as the letter touches his chest.

His hands start to crumple it as a stream of tears guides him downward toward what he must face.

As he turns it over, tears fall over the faded words his wife wrote.

“It’s been what? 23 y eAR s”

“I jus y u ay 27…”

“Every second of every day, you know…”

“I’m counting on you, Phil. Save her, Phil. We knew this would happen.”

“We saved tHo se woR Ds for tHi_s m    ent.”

“Here they are…”

“I        ou”

He crumples the letter, takes a deep breath, and removes his glasses to wipe the tears.

He closes his eyes and breathes in again.

Commotion comes through the windows. People screaming.

Phil regains his composure, jumping a few times before slamming his fist into the wall.

Then he looks back at his daughter.

She only looks at him.

No crying.

No words.

“Baby girl, we have to go.”

He walks to the window and back.

“Ok… Ok…”

“Food.”

He runs toward the kitchen, opens the fridge, looks through the pantry, and when he faces the table, everything is already prepared.

A heart saying, “thank you… you know…”

He starts to feel dizzy again. His hand lands on the table before another stream of tears takes over him.

“Abby…”

“Abby.”

“ABBY!”

He picks up a chair and throws it against the wall, kicking the leg of the table right after.

Looking at the ceiling.

“I can…”

“I can do this…”

“I can’t…”

“Why…”

He grabs the back of his head, pulling his hair.

“Sera!”

“Baby, I’m coming.”

He picks up the prepared meals and goes toward the room.

He stops by Sera’s room to pick up some clothes and shoves them into her backpack.

“Sera! We have to go, baby. Come on.”

When he walks into the room, she is already putting her crayons aside.

“Sera, Mommy…” Phil turns his head away from her and removes his glasses again to wipe his eyes. “Mommy will be away for a while, okay?”

“Mommy!”

“I know, baby.” He forces the glasses back on. “Come on. Daddy will take you piggyback, okay?”

“Mommy!”

“Come on, sweetie. Let’s go.”

He pulls the backpack into one hand and lifts her onto his back.

Sera’s arms close around his neck.

They rush toward the street-glow.

Cars block the exit from the walled section. People keep trying anyway, engines screaming, bumpers pushing into bumpers as if metal could make a way through metal.

Phil turns toward the garage.

A car slams into another near the entrance, trying to shove it aside.

“Baby, close your eyes, okay?”

Sera presses her face against his shoulder.

He reaches the garage and stops.

No exit.

Cars packed across every lane. Doors left open. Alarms crying into one another.

His hand goes to his pocket.

Nothing.

His phone is gone.

“The lab…”

He looks down the street.

“Sera, I’m going to run. Hold on tight.”

Phil runs.

Gunshots crack somewhere far away.

Then screams.

Then the screams change, spreading faster than the shots.

A blast rolls through the district behind him.

Sera tightens around his neck.

He does not stop.

By the time he reaches the lab, the doors are half-open and people are already moving the wrong way.

A colleague catches sight of him near the entrance.

“Phil? What are you doing here?”

“I have to get inside. I need to contact—”

“They’re coming. You know the procedure.”

The man puts a hand on his shoulder.

“Be safe. Don’t let them find you here.”

Another blast hits closer.

Phil pulls away.

Inside, the halls are too bright. Too clean. Too empty.

He reaches his office with Sera still on his back and pushes the door open hard enough to hit the wall.

“Sera, stay here, okay?”

He lowers her to the floor.

Then he starts tearing through the desk.

Chairs scrape. Drawers open. Papers spill. Something glass breaks against the floor.

“Fuck. Come on.”

A blast shakes the building.

He finds a pen.

Then paper.

His hand moves too fast for the words to look right.

He folds the page once.

Then again.

He crouches in front of Sera and slides it into one of her pockets.

“Keep this, okay?”

She looks at him.

He touches her face.

“Baby, come.”

He takes her into the device room.

The chair waits in the center.

Phil straps her in with shaking hands.

“Daddy?”

“I know. I know.”

He turns to the console.

“STOP!”

Two armed guards enter with their weapons raised.

“Step away from the machine!”

Phil keeps typing.

“Stop right now or we shoot!”

His fingers move faster.

“Who are you? What are you doing here?”

Phil’s hand stops over the keyboard.

“Who am I?”

He turns slowly.

“Who am I?”

The guards keep their weapons on him.

“This exists because of me.”

Sera starts crying.

“All of this. This room. This machine. These years.”

“My wife.”

A breath.

“My daughter.”

The guards hesitate.

“Sir…” one of them says, lowering his weapon slightly. “Calm down. We understand. Your daughter—”

“My daughter?”

Phil laughs once.

His hand drags up his face and stops against his forehead as his head tilts back.

“I lost my wife and my daughter to this piece of crap.”

His knees weaken.

He catches himself against the console.

“Abby…”

A breath catches.

“I won’t—”

The third soldier enters.

“What’s going on here?”

A shot cuts through the room.

Phil’s body folds forward.

His hand hits the key before he falls.
