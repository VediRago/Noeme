They sit on a bench along the plaza.

This is a district for rich people, high status, and heavy guards.

The colosseum stands ahead, crowded for the day’s fights. People gather around the entrance, waiting to see blood from a tradition older than most of the city.

The three urchins watch for prey.

A coin.

A bag.

Anything loose enough to disappear.

“Milo, you’re the distraction today,” Lina says, pushing him off the bench.

“Me?” Milo catches himself. “Why always me?”

Sera stands.

“Shut up, both of you.”

She nods toward the crowd.

“There. See that man?”

“The one in yellow drapes?” Lina asks, standing beside her.

“If you pay attention, he’s almost drunk.”

Sera turns back to them.

“I counted seven drinks.”

Milo is still on the bench, arms crossed.

“I’m not distraction.”

“Fine. I’ll go.”

Sera starts walking and disappears into the moving crowd.

Milo stands.

“Did she just…”

He looks around.

“Did she just leave us?”

“Get her!”

A shout cuts through the crowd.

People split as Sera ducks between legs and slips around bodies.

“I GOT IT!”

She blasts past them with a bag of coins in her hand.

Lina and Milo stand there, scratching their heads, as the rest of the crowd unravels behind her.
