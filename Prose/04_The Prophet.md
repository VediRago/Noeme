Abby sits in bed with the blankets covering her legs while Phil reads beside her.

The lampshade warms the room. The shutters soften the city outside until only the turning of pages remains.

“You know what I saw today?”

She drops her phone to her lap and looks down at him.

“What?” Phil asks, still fixed on the book.

“Sera.”

That makes him lower the page a little.

“She’s becoming such a beautiful girl.”

Phil lets the book fall to his chest and turns his head toward her.

“Yeah.”

A small smile reaches him before he can stop it.

“Her eyes are yours. Those green eyes are the only reason I fell for you.”

She kicks him under the blanket.

“The only reason, huh?”

She turns back to her phone.

“From my perspective, they weren’t your target most of the time.”

Phil cuts in before she can win too cleanly.

“Her hair, though. Do you have anyone in your family with red hair?”

Abby lowers the phone into her lap.

“Oh. Yeah. About that.”

She turns to him.

“I cheated on you.”

Phil shifts upright.

“What?”

“You wouldn’t shut up about the thing and saving people.” She exhales. “It got tiring. It’s like being with a prophet, and I don’t want that.”

She picks up her phone again.

Phil stares at her.

“What?”

Abby looks at him over the screen.

“Did it finally work? After all these years?”

Phil goes under the blankets.

“PHIL!”

She knees him away.

“Stop.”

He does not stop.

“Turn on the TV. Maybe one of your ads is on or something.”

She knees him again.

“Stop!”

Abby grabs the remote and turns on the TV while Phil starts tickling her.

“STOP!”

A voice enters the room.

“...if you have any of these symptoms, please call the number below.”

Abby hits the top of Phil’s head with her fist.

“I told you.”

Phil comes out from under the blankets with one hand on his head.

The broadcast continues.

“Do not leave your home. If you have a cough, weakness, difficulty breathing, or signs of blood—”

“Even in bed, we’re bombarded with tragedies.”

Abby lifts the remote to change the channel.

“They’re saying at work it may become a calamity,” Phil says.

“Oh, are they?”

She looks at him.

“What else do they tell the almighty savior of humanity?”

She changes the channel.

A bright field fills the screen.

A family runs through grass too green to be real.

“Ever thought of a new life?”

Phil puts his hand over the remote.

“Wait.”

Abby follows his eyes to the television.

“How about a new start on a lush green planet? Start again. Build again. Give your children a future beyond the walls.”

Abby exhales.

“One of yours?”

“They aren’t lush.”

A small laugh slips out of her.

“From prophet to god in less than fifteen minutes.”

Phil does not laugh with her.

His eyes stay on the screen.

The family keeps smiling.

Children run beneath a sky without smoke.

Phil lowers his voice.

“Seriously, Abby. We don’t even know where we send them.”

She looks at him.

“What?”

“We never hear from anyone. No one comes back. We think they go to other worlds, but to be honest, we don’t know for certain.”

Abby throws the remote at his chest.

“Are you kidding me?”

She gets out of bed and turns to face him.

“And you’re okay with that?”

Phil sits up.

“Abby—”

“Are you sending innocent people to death?”

Her hands land on her waist.

“I don’t know.”

He looks straight at her.

“What do you want me to do?”

The question leaves him with less force than he wanted.

He falls back against the bed and stares at the ceiling.

“Look at us.”

The advertisement keeps shining over the room.

“We’re safe.”

Abby says nothing.

“You wanted to leave that damn city, right?”

His voice thins.

“We’re safe.”
