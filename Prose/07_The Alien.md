Two siblings play under the shadow of the emperor’s statue.

The gardens around it are pristine green, sealed off by golden fences and watched from every side.

Lina and Milo drink from the nearby fountain before heading home.

The insulae connect through a dirt road hidden from the public path, and the children walk it kicking rocks, shoving each other, and laughing too loudly for a place that wants silence.

Then a beam of light cuts across the road.

A child appears in front of them.

Both siblings step back.

Milo stays closest.

Lina looks around, then rushes to the nearest tree and breaks off a stick.

Milo reaches out and touches the girl’s shoulder.

“I think it’s safe.”

Lina nearly slips against the wall as she hurries back.

“What is it?”

Milo shrugs.

“I’d say it’s a girl.”

“Of course it’s a girl.” Lina steps closer. “But where did she come from? The sky?”

They both look up.

Then back down.

The girl does not move.

“Are you all right?” Lina asks.

She touches the girl’s shoulder.

The girl looks directly at her.

Lina jumps back.

Milo looks up again.

“Do you think she can fly?”

“I don’t know.” Lina leans closer. “But look at what she’s wearing.”

Milo circles the girl.

“Is that a bag?”

Lina takes the backpack before the question finishes.

She gives herself no time to feel guilty before opening it.

“She has tiny hidden pockets in her clothes!” Milo says.

He starts checking them.

The girl only stands there, looking from one child to the other without saying a word.

“She’s an alien, Milo.”

Lina spreads crayons and two sandwiches across the dirt.

“Look.”

Milo crouches over them.

“Maybe she fell out of a passing car.”

“And landed here?”

“Maybe that’s why Father always tells us to lock the doors.”

“Must be.”

Milo pulls out a folded piece of paper.

“Hey. Look here.”

An old man slows on the path.

“What are you two doing to that poor little girl?”

“We think she’s an alien,” Lina says. “She fell from the sky right here.”

The old man looks at the girl.

Then at the scattered crayons.

“You kids…”

He keeps walking.

“Milo,” Lina whispers, “take the paper to the scriptorium machine. Maybe there’s a clue.”

Milo grabs the girl’s arm and pulls her along.

“Come on, little one. We won’t let you get lost. We’ll find your father.”

The girl follows.

She does not cry.

At the scriptorium machine, Lina smooths the paper against the scanner while Milo starts pressing buttons.

The machine clicks.

A voice answers.

“Error. Unknown.”

Lina opens her mouth.

Then leans close to Milo.

“She’s an alien.”

Milo presses another button.

“Error. Unknown.”

Another.

“Error. Unknown.”

They keep going.

Citizens pass by without giving much attention to the two little monkeys beating a public machine while a silent girl stands beside them.

Then the machine stutters.

The voice changes.

“Please take care of my daughter. Her name is Sera, and she is from another world.”

Both siblings jump back.

They stare at her.

Milo points.

“You are an alien.”

A passerby stops for a second.

Then he shrugs and continues on his way.

“We need to tell Mom,” Lina says.

“Put her on my back,” Milo says. “Let’s run as fast as we can.”

Milo takes off with Sera clinging to him.

Lina stays behind for a few steps, looking around to see if anyone noticed.

Then she dashes after them.

They are still far from the door when both siblings start shouting.

“MOOOOM!”

Elena appears barefoot, one hand over her eyes against the light.

“MOM!”

She starts running toward them.

“What’s wrong? What happened?”

The children stop in front of her, both trying to catch their breath.

Elena looks past them.

Her voice softens.

“Who is this little girl?”

Milo points at Sera.

“Mom, you won’t believe this—”

Lina cuts him off.

“She’s an alien!”
