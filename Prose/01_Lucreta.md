# Prologue

## Lucreta

She sits quietly on the corner of her bed. The room dims with passing clouds through the steel framed window. Vera arranges the headdress of white orchids and rosemary, carefully weaving them into her crimson hair.

Her fingers trace the ornate mahogany at the foot of the bed, following the intricate curves carved into the wood from where they begin to where they end. Her hand stops. Her ear turns toward the closed window. Beyond it, carriage wheels roll across stone. Voices follow.

She moves her hand to her waist. Standing, she gently pushes Vera aside. Her stride stiffens, but it is barely noticed. Vera speaks no words, instead dropping her head as Lucreta passes by.

The outside brims with life. Carriages rolling across cobbled streets. The suits and dresses of passing lives. Wooden caskets stacked with bottles. Baskets overflowing with fruit. The parasols. The hats. The bags. The canes. Lucreta keeps looking as Vera gently pulls her away from the window.

The street slips from view. Her eyes lift. Above the rooftops, a bright blue sky. She closes her eyes.

A gentle knock stirs the silence, though the voice that follows carries far less patience.

“Lucreta! Are you ready yet? The guests have been waiting nearly an hour already. Surely you do not intend to leave your groom languishing from boredom before he can even marry you.”

When she opens them again, her pale-mint eyes anchor her reflection, erasing everything else. Slowly, the rest of her comes into focus around them—the ivory folds of her wedding gown blooming toward the floor, lace contours contrasting the vivid colors her eyes have lost.

Vera's gaze lingers upon the reflection for only a moment before falling away. Her hands return to the delicate strands cascading over Lucreta's shoulders.

“Do not tell me you have grown fearful of womanhood at the final hour, dear. You linger so long one might think this marriage burdens you terribly.”

The room settles into silence.

Lucreta's gaze remains fixed.

“No, Vera. Quite the contrary.”

A faint smile touches her lips.

“Nothing could—”

The words break apart beneath a sudden spasm in her chest. She turns sharply, one hand catching the edge of the table as the cough tears free from her throat.

For a moment she cannot breathe.

When it passes, she draws in a slow, careful breath.

“Nothing could mar this day,” she says quietly. “There is nothing I have ever desired more than this hour.”

The cough returns before the final word has fully left her mouth. Sharper this time. A faint stain blooms against her lips. Her eyes widen. A hand rises quickly to her mouth. She wipes the blood away before turning her palm against the fabric of her gown, as though it might disappear there.

Only then remembering what she is wearing.

Vera's hands still.

For the briefest moment, worry surfaces across her reflection. Then something practiced settles over it instead. A careful smile. Gentle enough to preserve the moment rather than shatter it.

“So—”

A brush slides through her hair.

“How long before I may tend to the child you are certain to bless us with?”

A subtle tilt of Lucreta's chin.

“And yet you speak of that child so readily.”

A glance toward Vera.

“As though you have spent longer preparing for its coming than my own mother ever spent preparing for me.”

Vera's smile weakens briefly, taking much of the room's fragile warmth with it.

Lucreta watches her.

A knock bursts through the moment.

“Lucreta! Your father will grow tired of the groom before you are even married. Come out, girl—at this rate you shall emerge old and wrinkled before the ceremony begins.”

Vera slides a hand across Lucreta's shoulder and down her arm before stepping away.

“Go, dear. No one deserves this moment more than you.”

A tear slips down her cheek. She wipes it away before it can fall any further. Turning slightly, she hides her reflection from view.

Lucreta straightens slowly and gathers the skirts of her gown with practiced grace.

A blur halts her step.

Again.

She continues toward the door. From the corner of her eye, a faint trail of her reflection lingers a fraction too long.

As though it too has faltered.

Her hand closes around the handle.

Silence.

“L—ta!”

Her fingers loosen. Sliding along polished brass.

“—creta!”

The room lurches sideways. A violent knock tears through everything else, swallowing her senses in a single sharp impact of sound.

Her pale-mint eyes remain fixed and hollow as her body crumples across the floor.
