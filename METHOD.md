# Method

NOEME is built through layered documents that look at the same story from different distances.

The goal is not to repeat the same information four times. The goal is to view the same material through four different lenses: act guide, blueprint, prose, and screenplay.

## The Pipeline

### Act Guide

The Act Guide establishes the emotional weather of the act before any individual scene is examined.

It explains the act's purpose, atmosphere, scenery, emotional movement, visual language, and major motifs. The goal is not to tell the story scene by scene, but to help the reader understand what the act should feel like as a whole.

### Blueprint

Blueprints zoom into individual scenes.

They focus on direction, visual attention, reveal structure, pacing, and emotional progression. A blueprint is not concerned with production formatting or prose readability. Its purpose is to establish how the scene works.

### Prose

The prose version presents the story in its most readable form.

Unlike a screenplay, prose is not restricted by production formatting, camera language, or character cues. It allows atmosphere, emotion, rhythm, and sensory experience to take priority.

The purpose of prose is to experience the story itself.

### Screenplay

The screenplay translates the material into a production-facing format.

It focuses on image, action, dialogue, timing, and execution. Information that may exist comfortably in prose must now be communicated through what can be seen, heard, or performed.

The purpose of the screenplay is to communicate the story clearly to production.

## What Each Layer Answers

**Act Guide:**  
What am I about to experience?

**Blueprint:**  
How is this scene directed?

**Prose:**  
What does this story feel like when read?

**Screenplay:**  
How is this story communicated for production?

## Why This Matters

NOEME depends on recognition before explanation.

The documents follow the same principle. The reader first encounters the feeling of the act, then the scene direction, then the readable story, then the production-facing screenplay. The method is explained after the evidence exists.

This makes the portfolio more than a collection of story files. It shows how the pilot moves from emotional architecture to scene direction, from readable prose to screenplay execution.
